Written by Akezhan Rakishev    Digital Narrative Final Draft

Uncle's bike | Digital Narrative

Screen Resolution

The video plays at a maximum of 1920 x 1080 pixels at 60 frames per second which provides the audience with convenient and high quality. 60 FPS (frames per second) allows the audience to observe the film smoothly.

Technology choice

After surfing among many video editing software, I decided to use iMovie because of its simplicity and broad functionality. iMovie provides useful tools to process videos such as color saturation, the brightness of video and images. In the first couple of scenes, I rose and slowed down the speed of the videos. Besides video editing, iMovie has handy features for audio editing. One of the most influential and impressive features was the smooth transition of audio volume between the scenes.

Topic and conflict

Several important themes and topics intersect in the digital narrative. The central topics are the waste of time and energy. The protagonist experience internal crisis when riding on public transport as it takes his time and enthusiasm. Crowd, noise and slow commute create a serious challenge for the main character. The conflict reinforces and forces the main character to surrender due to the situation.

Storyboard

Before discussing the storyboard, it is important to identify the term timeline-based text.

Timeline-based text is one of the audience experiences in a linear sequence to lead your audience along a path.

Since the digital narrative is timeline-based, I decided to organize by storyboard by the sequence of scenes and events for the exposition, rising action, climax, falling action and the resolution. Then, I wrote the script for each of the main parts of the story and made a research on the representation, shooting angles, incidental music, and transitions.

Shooting angles

The digital narrative has diverse types of shots, shooting angles what makes the film seem engaging and interesting.

Sound effects

There are three main musical compositions used in the digital narrative. Even though all of them were much quieter than the authorâ€™s voice, they nicely fitted with the speech of the author and so they were able to share my feelings and film tone.

The first one is 'Get Lucky' by Daft Punk feat. Pharrell Williams, Nile Rodgers. The music was playing at the exposition part when I tried to provide the audience with my background as a simple guy who finds a job opportunity.

When describing the conflict and crisis of the digital narrative, I chose the 'Springtime' composition by Luke Faulkner to reinforce the emotional appeal on the audience. The piano composition is very applicable when showing sad and sorrowful scenes.

The final musical composition that I have used for the climax, falling action and resolution was the 'Clear Concept' by Benoit Jego-Florent Duclos. This song goes well with the spirit of rebirth and gradual inspiration. When my uncle bought a bike for me, I started to gradually get the interest in life and feel
inspired. I was able to share these feelings, progression and experience through that music.

Final thoughts

The creation and the development of the digital narrative was a great challenge as well as experience for me. I was able to demonstrate and apply my creativity, skills to build a powerful movie.
